Adjective order (֭ſɭƨᴜ ſɭᴜ }ʃꞇ j͑ʃɔ ſɭ,ᴜ j͑ʃᴜꞇ)

 

The origin of this was to distinguish literal adjectives and symbolic adjectives as older versions of Iikrhian had a lower amount of vocabulary. The reason why the symbolic adjectives come after is because they are less important in meaning compared to the root. In the past as well, the number of words that could be stacked was much higher although this changed so that the only time this happened was with words what already had a modifier so that new words wouldnt have two and only one. For example ŋᷠɜⅎᶗ‹ ſɭэ j͐ʃꞇȝ ꞁȷ̀ᴜꞇ is a sandwhich while ŋᷠɜⅎᶗ‹ ſɭэ j͐ʃꞇȝ ꞁȷ̀ᴜꞇ ſ̀ȷᴜ ſɭɔͷ̗ ꞁȷ̀ᴜꞇ is a philly cheesesteak and it is a subset of sandwhich. Whats interesting about this is that this was a natural change meaning that this happened unconciously as i used to the language. Antoher part of this that is also natural is how what words were used in compounding changed as well where in the past compounds used to sould like whole sentences. Then, it became only adjectives with the - j͑ʃᴜꞇ suffix. Part of this was because in the past, Iikrhian did not distinguish adjectives from verbs and most words were verbs. That old version of Iikrhian also used classifiers intensily which could also be the reason for the development of the symbolic adjectives.

Personal Names |ſɭɔ˞ᴜ ſɭᴜ|

 

The concept of names were loaned back in sixth grade from the Warriors clans. However, this has changed over time as names became quite long.

Names are still composed of two separate parts. However, they can be shortened

 

j͑ʃɔ ſȷэⅎ ſɭᴜ ɭʃᴜ j͑ʃɔᶗ‹ |j͑ʃɔ ſȷэⅎ x ſɭᴜ ɭʃᴜ j͑ʃɔᶗ‹| > j͑ʃɔʞ ɭʃᴜ j͑ʃɔᶗ‹

 

In the past, a person would have 5 different names which were your nickname, first name, group name, parent name, and family name.

 

Now however, only nicknames and first names are used. The others are still used by some however.

Nicknames are just the shortened normal names.

 

The use of clipped Iikrhian words is not limited to names. It is also used for some new words as well. Similarly names may be translated this way.

 

The first part of a name is always an adjective, so it has no -(s)ai suffix. The last part of a name is always a noun, so no -(a)ni suffix.

Hrlɔʔ prgap / Reverse words |ſɭɔʞ ſןɜ ᶅſwƽ ꞁȷ̀ᴜꞇ|

 

Hrlɔʔ prgap (Khmu word) can be found to some extant in Iikrhian mainly in word play. This practice originated from Khmer (Unsure of the Khmer word). It is often supplemented by a type of reduplication* found in Khmer. So words like ſɭᴜ ɭʃᴜ ſɭᴜ would be reversed as ſɭƨᴜ ſɭᴜ. Although these have similar meaning, it does not always have to be this way.

 

*The type of reduplication that was loaned is generally not used in poetry in Iikrhian as using different words is preferred. In fact the type of reduplication thing is considered a part of the vowel shift game mentioned earlier.

 

 

Profanity

 

Iikrhian is more likely to have taboo words, there are unsuprisingly little profanity. Most of the time, a word is loaned from another conlang like Gulundic Skana or Verdurian Futaš or Wezayek Fujazi. This is used since it can be offensive for another people group which has been important to the Iikrhians for a while. In fact, there is a popular Loishə song called Fiishasi Fiitashai based from it.

 

However, if you want to use an Iikrhian word, you can say ı],ɹ ı],ɹ ŋᷠᴜȝ or normal-mind which just means stupid in the same way mousebrain does in Warriors. Aside from that there are not many ways to do so as this is not a core part of Iikrhian culture.

 

A true way to be offensive is to make fun of their name by using a similar sounding word that implies something rude.

 

However, in some cases, breaking taboo is offensive. It is not recommended though as they serve their purpose for protection.
