Observer Marker (ᶅſэ)

 

ᶅſэ is an observing marker meaning someone else is involved or simply someone else. this is related to twa (-п́-) plus a negative. it usually translates to not me.

 

ꞁȷ̀ɹ ʃᴜ ɭʃп́ᴜ ᶅſэ

to see someone else's self.

ꞁȷ̀ɹ ʃᴜ ɭʃᴜƴ ᶅſэ

to see yourself as someone else.

 

ꞁȷ̀ɹ ʃᴜ ᶅſэ ſȷᴜͷ̗

to see the book through something else.

ꞁȷ̀ɹ ʃᴜ ſȷᴜͷ̗ ᶅſэ

to see the book (that is not related to you).

 

ʃэ ֭ſɭɜ ᶅſɔ ᶅſэ

to expect / to predict for someone else.

 

j͐ʃэ }ʃᴜ means by others and sometimes acts as a passive. in some ways it is the negative of j͐ʃэ }ʃᴜ as the main focus is from them to you rather than from you to them.

 

j͐ʃэ }ʃᴜ ɭʃɔ ſ͕ɭᴜƴ

to require / by someone else need.

ɭʃɔ ſ͕ɭᴜƴ ᶅſэ

to want someone to.

 

j͐ʃэ }ʃᴜ ſɟᴜ ŋᷠɹ / j͐ʃэ }ʃᴜ ꞁȷ̀ᴜ ɽ͑ʃ'ɜ ֭ſɭᴜ

Time is seen as bottom to top like the script. Timelines themself are said to appear linear, although the time it is created is random. It is believed that this randomness of occurences is infinite and as new worlds begin, old ones become less frequently occuring.

 

Because of this, new and old days are represented as next and previous days only compared to other languages. Note that next and previous is more imagined similar to a stack.

 

ſɭɔ˞ɜ ɽ͑ʃ'ᴜ j͑ʃᴜꞇ j͑ʃƨᴜ ſȷͷ̗ɹ

However, despite this up and out are generally considered newer while down and in are considered older which matches soils and tree growth as well as the script. The reason why the time is different is due to how time is percieved by Iikrhians as being created at random whilst appearing chronological.

 

Time is generally considered as opening/turning on or closing/turning off.

Continuing sentences

 

In spoken and sometimes written Iikrhian, the last part of the sentence is repeated to continue the conversation. Like this.

 

I went. Went to the house. Went to the house to get. In the house get the food

 

Repetition taboo

 

Repitition is not productive at all in Iikrhian with one exception.

 

A taboo can be reversed if it is said twice. This is only possible in nouns as adjectives might be rendered as a compound. Moreover, this deletes the word from the sentence meaning that the position will have to be replaced with a filler word or ilommitted if it includes a verb. Similarly, this means that the word cannot have adjuncts decribing it.
