ſɭɹ- is used to represent using something to do something.

ſɭɹ ſ͔ɭᴜ ᶅſɔ ꞁȷ̀ɔ ꞁȷ̀ɹ ſɭˬꞇᴜ ſɭᴜͷ̗ ꞁȷ̀ɜ j͑ʃ'ɔ ſȷᴜͷ̗ ⟅

Can read in Iikrhian.

ꞁȷ̀ɜ j͑ʃ'ɔ ſȷᴜͷ̗ ſɭɹ ſ͔ɭᴜ ᶅſɔ ꞁȷ̀ɔ ꞁȷ̀ɹ ſɭˬꞇᴜ ſɭᴜͷ̗ ſ͕ɭᴜꞇ j͑ʃɹ ſȷᴜͷ̗ ⟅

can-VBZ-book use-language e iikrhia kal this.ADJZ DIM.book.

Can read this story in Iikrhian.

From / Passive voice

Connected to a noun, j͐ʃэ- means by/from. Connected to an verb, it is passive voice.

Made by / Made from

There is not really distinction made in Iikrhian about made by or made from. Both can use j͐ʃэ j͑ʃɔ-. However, this makes sense since creations are produced by someone like wood is produced by trees. To speak about someone making someone out of something, then say this.

You can use j͑ʃɔ- or use a full sentence.

j͑ʃɔ ŋᷠᴜƴ j͐ʃэ j͑ʃɔ ſɭɔ ֭ſɭw ſɭᶗ‹ᴜ ⟅

POSS-human from-POSS-wood knife.

The person's wooden knife.

j͐ʃэ j͑ʃɔ ſɭɔ ֭ſɭw ſɭʞɹ ſɭᶗ‹ᴜ ⺓ ŋᷠᴜƴ ⟅

from-POSS wood make knife SUBCL human.

The person made a knife out of wood.

j͐ʃэ ſɭɹ- can be used for made out of when the subject is taboo.

However, it can also be distinguished by whether the adjective is on the front or back of a word.

ſ͔ɭэ j͐ʃэ j͑ʃɔ ſᶘᴜ

wind from-POSS-water

Wind made out of water

j͐ʃэ j͑ʃɔ ſᶘᴜ ſ͔ɭɹ

from-of-water wind

Water made by the wind

You can also just use j͐ʃэ-. However, it depends on what is said.

j͐ʃэ ſᶘᴜ ſ͔ɭэ

from-water wind

Wind from water (Usually from it and not made by)

In can be distinguished from on this way as well.

ɭl̀э ſɭᴜ ſᶘɹ ֭ſɭɹƽ

in-fish gill

gill on the outside (although attached) of fish / external gill

ſᶘɹ ֭ſɭɹƽ ɭl̀э ſɭᴜ

gill in-fish

gill inside fish / internalised gill / book lung

ɭl̀э j͑ʃ'ᴜ ſᶘᴜȝ

Can

ꞁȷ̀ɜ- can be placed on a noun like so.

ſ͔ɭɔȝ ꞁȷ̀ɜ ſɭп́w ſɟwͷ̗ ɭʃꞇ j͐ʃ ꞁȷ̀ɜ ſɭᴜ ſɟꞇ ⺓ ſ͕ȷᴜc̗ ſɭɜ ⟅

eat can-rabbit can-chicken piise cat.

Cats can eat rabbits and can eat chicken.

ꞁȷ̀ɜ ſɭɹ ſᶘɹᶗ‹ ɭʃɜȝ ꞁȷ̀ᴜ ŋᷠɹ ɭʃᴜ ſɭɜ ſɭᴜ j͑ʃᴜ }ʃɔ j͑ʃп́ꞇ ſɭᴜ j͐ʃ ꞁȷ̀ꞇ }ʃᴜƽ ꞁȷ̀ᴜ }ʃꞇ ⟅

֭ſɭᴜ ɭʃᴜ }ʃɔ ſɭᴜ j͑ʃᴜ ֭ſɭw }ʃᴜ ſȷɹ ŋᷠɹ ⟅

rain NEG-then dark cloud.

It rains due to the dark clouds.

ꞁȷ̀ᴜ ſᶘᴜ ꞁȷ̀ɹ ʃᴜ ſɭᴜ j͑ʃᴜ j͑ʃ'ᴜ ɭʃᴜ ֭ſɭᴜȝ ⟅

j͑ʃ'ᴜ ɭʃᴜ ֭ſɭᴜȝ }ʃɔ ֭ſɭᴜ j͑ʃᴜ ꞁȷ̀ɹ ʃᴜ ⟅

Possession

j͑ʃɔ- can be animate or inanimate based on order. Order can also be used for alienability.

ɭʃᴜ ŋᷠᴜ j͑ʃɔ ɭʃƽᴜͷ̗

hand POSS-3s

Their hand (Attached)

j͑ʃɔ ɭʃƽᴜͷ̗ ɭʃᴜ ŋᷠᴜ

POSS-3S hand

Their hand (Distached)

There is a difference between using j͑ʃɔ- and -j͑ʃᴜꞇ for possession.

j͑ʃɔ ſɭɔƴ ſ͔ɭɔƽ

POSS-2s room

Your room (Owned)

ſɭɔƴ ꞁȷ̀ᴜꞇ ſ͔ɭɔƽ

2s-ADJV room

Your room (Related)

The word have (ſ͕ɭwȝ) is different from ꞁȷ̀ɹ-.

ſ͕ɭwȝ ꞁȷ̀ᴜͷ̗ ſɭᴜ j͑ʃ'ᴜ j͑ʃᴜꞇ ſɟᴜ ſ͔ɭᴜꞇ ſ͕ɭꞇ ⟅

have-l six-ADJZ tool-play

Own six toys (Not necessarily have right now)

ſɭᴜ j͑ʃ'ᴜ j͑ʃᴜꞇ ꞁȷ̀ɹ ſɟᴜ ſ͔ɭᴜꞇ ſ͕ɭꞇ ⟅

six-sai with-tool-play

Have six toys (Right now and not necessarily own them)

ꞁȷ̀ɹ- can also mean a part of something.

ſןᴜ ſ͔ɭᴜ ꞁȷ̀ɹ ֭ſɭꞇ ʃɜ ɽ͑ʃ'ꞇ ⟅

Animal that has feathers as a part of it.

About / Relating to

Use j͑ʃɹ ᶅſɔ for when there is a topic.

j͑ʃɹ ᶅſɔ ꞁȷ̀ɔ ꞁȷ̀ɹ ſɭˬꞇᴜ ſɭᴜ }ʃᴜȝ ɭʃꞇȝ

about-e iikrhia congruent this

In Iikrhian, it is the same as this.

Particles

You can use ſ͕ɭᴜꞇ and ŋᷠᴜꞇ for classification.

ſɭэ ſɭɔ ſ͕ɭᴜꞇ ɽ͑ʃ'ᴜ j͑ʃꞇc̗ ɭʃᴜ

Observer Marker

ᶅſэ is an observing marker meaning someone else is involved or simply someone else. This is related to ɭʃп́ᴜ and ɭʃᴜƴ (-п́-).

ʃэ ֭ſɭɜ ᶅſɔ ᶅſэ

to expect / to predict for someone else.

j͐ʃэ }ʃᴜ means by others and sometimes acts as a passive.

j͐ʃэ }ʃᴜ ɭʃɔ ſ͕ɭᴜƴ

To require / By someone else need.

ɭʃɔ ſ͕ɭᴜƴ ᶅſэ

To want someone to.

j͐ʃэ }ʃᴜ ſɟᴜ ŋᷠɹ / j͐ʃэ }ʃᴜ ꞁȷ̀ᴜ ɽ͑ʃ'ɜ ֭ſɭᴜ

To be killed.

ꞁȷ̀ᴜ ɽ͑ʃ'ɜ ֭ſɭᴜ ᶅſэ

Kill (someone else did it).

j͑ʃ'ɔɔ˞ ꞁȷ̀ᴜ ɽ͑ʃ'ɜ ֭ſɭᴜ

Cause to kill.

Time and Direction

ꞁȷ̀ɔⅎ- and ɽ͑ʃ'ᴜ- also ꞁȷ̀ɔȝ and ſɭᴎɔ and ɽ͑ʃ'ᴜ ŋᷠɹ are used to tell relative time while ſɭw ŋᷠw and ſɟꞇƴ and ſ͕ɭᴜ ᶅſᴜ tell the actual time.

ꞁȷ̀ɔⅎ ֭ſɭᴜ ɭʃᴜ

It had rained (and had stopped).

ɽ͑ʃ'ᴜ ֭ſɭᴜ ɭʃᴜ

Yet to rain.

ſɭw ŋᷠw ɽ͑ʃ'ᴜ ŋᷠɹ ſ͔ɭᴜꞇ ſ͕ɭꞇ

Back then, will play

ſ͕ɭᴜ ᶅſᴜ ſɭᴎɔ ſ͔ɭᴜꞇ ſ͕ɭꞇ

In the future, will be playing

ſɟꞇƴ ꞁȷ̀ɔȝ ſ͔ɭᴜꞇ ſ͕ɭꞇ

In the present, have played already

ſɟꞇƴ ſɭп́ɔ ſ͔ɭᴜꞇ ſ͕ɭꞇ

Right now, I am currently playing

Time is generally considered as opening/turning on or closing/turning off.

ꞁȷ̀ᴜ ſ̀ȷɔ j͑ʃƨꞇ ſȷɔ ֭ſɭɹ

open time

Time starts

Direction was almost always based on north/south/east/west in the past. However, there are now righthand/lefthand/front/back directions.
