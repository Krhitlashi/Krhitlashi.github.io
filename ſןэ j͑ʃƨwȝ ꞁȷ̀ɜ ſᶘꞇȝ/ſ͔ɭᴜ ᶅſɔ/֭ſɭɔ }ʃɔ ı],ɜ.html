Introduction (֭ſɭɔ }ʃɔ ı],ɜ)

The language spoken in Aisiakrhi, my personal conlang. The language has been shaped a lot by tsacek (hasaqai taboo), adjective ordering in compounds, and the creation of words and roots in general. It also has gone through a thorough removal of loanwords and calques to make the language more specific to myself. As a result, this language is different from the one used months ago. The register system in Iikrhian arose from avoidance taboo via tsacek where I would use different sets of vocabulary for different people. Also some new and old sound changes have started occuring again. Thus once again it is a language in the process of transition and change. One sound change that has happened as a result of the merger of finals into velar consonants is the process of paragoge. Words that would produce homonyms had an -a suffixed to them. After p or m, it would become -u (Later -ii). Also, u used to be compressed, though during the loss of voiced plossives which initially caused the vowel afterwards to become rounded which meant the decompression of u. Something cool and important to know is that language is highly influenced by its speaker and Iikrhian is not any different. It contains words for things you wouldn't expect to have words or roots for.

Dialects (j͑ʃɹ ſ͔ɭᴜ ᶅſɔ)

(Cannonical lore for Shumekayne as well as some other stuff. Not true for Earth). Throughout the years diifferent dialects emerged especially through Iikrhian isolation which affected Aisiakrhi internally as well. However, due to the rapid decline of Aisiakrhi's power in the Shumekayne as well as in other places, dialectal variation only survives in Iikrhian Iikrhiahrhiisla. The rerise of Aisiakrhi was very quick and limited in size, so there wasn’t much time for change. Most dialects also have nearly the same phonology as standard Iikrhian due to isolation and purification. However, vocabulary was tolerated. This is one of the reason why many minority languages within Iikrhian territory survive (Compared to others of the Shumekayne) although influenced by Iikrhian. Note that not all words are dialectal; some are just variants used in the main language. However, some dialectal variants were loaned into main language and vise versa, so it is unclear where many variants originated.
